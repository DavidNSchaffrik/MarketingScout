searchlogic said:
Many people still focus only on metrics like DA.

But in real SEO, niche relevance plays a bigger role.

Here’s why:

• A relevant backlink helps search engines understand your website’s topic.
• Contextual links look more natural and trustworthy.
• Relevant sites send targeted traffic not random visitors.
• Long-term ranking stability improves with topical alignment.

High metrics are good.
But relevance + quality is stronger.

What matters more in your experience — metrics or relevance?
Click to expand...
I agree relevance carries more weight long term, but I’ve noticed something interesting. A relevant link from a weak, thin site often does less than a slightly broader but editorially strong placement from a trusted publication. For me, the real differentiator isn’t just niche match, it’s whether the linking page itself has topical depth and audience engagement.

In other words, page-level context sometimes matters more than domain-level metrics. Curious if others evaluate links at the page level rather than just site niche.

I believe the DA metric is outdated, and there’s no real point in relying on it. It’s easy to manipulate.

Instead, you should focus on relevance, the backlink profile of your potential donor, how traffic is distributed across the site, what anchor texts are used in its backlinks, whether there are signs of artificial traffic, traffic dynamics over the last two years, and the ratio of incoming to outgoing links.
